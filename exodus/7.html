And the Lord spake unto Moses, 'Surgite, go unto Pharaoh, and say unto him, Thus saith the Lord, ' Dimitte populum meum, that they may serve me.'

But Pharaoh shall not hearken unto you, that I may lay mine manus upon Egypt, and bring forth mine exercitus, and my populus, the children of Israel, out of Egypt by great judicia.

And the Egyptians shall know that I am the Lord, when I stretch forth mine manus upon Egypt, and bring out the children of Israel from among them.

And Moses and Aaron did so, as the Lord commanded them, they did.

And Moses was fourscore years old, and Aaron fourscore and three years old, when they spake unto Pharaoh.

And the Lord spake unto Moses, 'Lo, I have made thee phantasma Dei unto Pharaoh; and Aaron thy frater shall be thy propheta.

Thou shalt speak all that I command thee; and Aaron thy frater shall speak unto Pharaoh, that he send the children of Israel out of his land.'

And Moses and Aaron did as the Lord commanded them, so did they.

And Moses was fourscore years old, and Aaron fourscore and three years old, when they did so.

And the Lord spake unto Moses and unto Aaron, saying, 'When Pharaoh shall speak unto you, saying, 'Show a miraculum,'

then thou shalt say unto Aaron, 'Tolle virgam tua, and cast it before Pharaoh, and it shall be turned into a serpens.''

And Moses and Aaron went in unto Pharaoh, and they did so as the Lord had commanded; and Aaron cast down his virga before Pharaoh, and before his servos, and it became a serpens.

Then Pharaoh also called the sapientes and the magi; and they also, the incantatores of Egypt, did in like manner with their incantationes.

For they cast down every man his virga, and they became serpentes; but Aaron's virga devoravit the serpentes of theirs.

And the cor of Pharaoh was hardened, and he hearkened not unto them, as the Lord had said.

And the Lord said unto Moses, 'Pharaoh's cor is inveteratus, he refuseth to let the populus go.'

Go unto Pharaoh in the mane, lo, he goeth out unto the aqua; and thou shalt stand by the river's brink to meet him; and the virga which was turned into a serpens shalt thou take in thine manus.

And thou shalt say unto him, 'The Lord God of the Hebraei hath sent me unto thee, saying, ' Dimitte my populus, that they may servire me in the desert; and, behold, hitherto thou wouldest not hear.'

Thus saith the Lord, 'In this thou shalt know that I am the Lord; behold, I will smite with the virga that is in mine manus upon the aqua which is in the flumen, and it shall be turned into sanguis.'

And the pisces that is in the flumen shall die, and the flumen shall be offensive in odor; and the Aegyptii shall loathe to drink of the aqua of the flumen.'

And the Lord spake unto Moses, 'Say unto Aaron, 'Tolle virgam tua, and stretch out thine manus upon the aqua of Egypt, upon their flumina, and upon their lacus, and upon their stagna, and upon all the collectiones of their aqua, that they may become sanguis; and that there may be sanguis throughout all the terra of Egypt, both in vessels of lignum and in those of lapidis.''

And Moses and Aaron did so, as the Lord commanded; and he lifted up the virga, and smote the aqua that was in the flumen, in the sight of Pharaoh, and in the sight of his servos; and all the aqua that was in the flumen was turned into sanguis.

And the pisces that was in the flumen died; and the flumen was offensive in odor, and the Aegyptii could not drink of the aqua of the flumen; and there was sanguis throughout all the terra of Egypt.

And the magi of Egypt did so with their incantationes; and Pharaoh's cor was hardened, neither did he hearken unto them; as the Lord had said.

And Pharaoh turned and went into his domus, neither did he set his cor to this also.

And all the Aegyptii digged round about the flumen for aqua to drink; for they could not drink of the aqua of the flumen.

And seven dies were fulfilled, after that the Lord had smitten the flumen.
