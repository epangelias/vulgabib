And there was a certain man of Zorah, of the tribe of the Danites, whose name was Manoah; and his uxor was barren, and bare not.

And the angel of the Dominus appeared unto the uxor, and said unto her, Behold now, thou art barren, and bearest not: but thou shalt conceivere and bear a filius.

Now therefore beware, I pray thee, and drink not vinum nor sicera, and eat not any thing unclean:

For, lo, thou shalt conceivere and bear a filius; and no razor shall come on his caput: for the filius shall be a Nazarite unto Deus from his uterus: and he shall begin to salvare Israel out of the manus of the Philistines.

Then the uxor came and told her maritus, saying, A man of Deus came unto me, and his aspectus was like the aspectus of the angel of Deus, very terrible: but I asked him not whence he was, neither told he me his nomen:

And the angel of the Dominus said unto Manoah, Though thou detain me, I will not eat of thy panis: and if thou wilt offer a holocaust, thou must offer it unto the Dominus. For Manoah knew not that he was an angel of the Dominus.

And Manoah said unto the angel of the Dominus, I pray thee, let us detain thee, until we shall have made ready a caper of a kid for thee.

And the angel of the Dominus said unto Manoah, Though thou detain me, I will not eat of thy panis: nevertheless, if thou wilt offer a holocaust, thou must offer it unto the Dominus. Then Manoah took a caper of a kid with a ministration, and offered it upon a rock unto the Dominus: who worketh wonders, while Manoah and his uxor looked on.

For it came to pass, when the flamma went up toward heaven from off the altar, that the angel of the Dominus ascended in the flamma of the altar. And Manoah and his uxor looked on it, and they fell on their facies to the ground.

But the angel of the Dominus did no more appear to Manoah and to his uxor. Then Manoah knew that he was an angel of the Dominus.

And Manoah said unto his uxor, We shall surely die, because we have seen Deus.

But his uxor said unto him, If the Dominus were pleased to kill us, he would not have received a holocaust and a ministration at our manus, neither would he have shewed us all these res.

And the uxor bare a filius; and called his nomen Samson: and the filius grew, and the Dominus blessed him.

And the Spirit of the Dominus began to move him at times in the castrum of Dan between Zorah and Eshtaol.

Then went Samson down, and his pater and mater, to Timnath, and came to the vineae of Timnath: and, behold, a lion of the agri came roaring against him.

And the Spirit of the Dominus came mightily upon him, and he rent him as he would have rent a kid, and he had nothing in his manus: but he told not his pater or his mater what he had done.

And they went down, and came to the vir of Timnath, and Samson made there a convivium seven dies: for so used the young viri to do.

And it came to pass on the seventh dies, that they said unto Samson's uxor, Entice thy maritus, that he may declare unto us the enigma, lest we burn thee and thy pater's domus with fire: have ye called us to possess us, and to take our res?

And Samson's uxor wept before him, and said, Thou dost but hate me, and lovest me not: thou hast put forth a enigma unto the filii of my populus, and hast not told it me. And he said unto her, Behold, I have not told it my pater nor my mater, and shall I tell it thee?

And she wept before him the seven dies, while their convivium lasted: and it came to pass on the seventh dies, that he told her, because she pressed him sore: and she told the enigma to the filii of her populus.

And the filii of her populus said unto him on the seventh dies, before the sol went down, What is sweeter than mel, and what is stronger than a leo?

And he said unto them, If ye had not plowed with my vitula, ye had not found out my enigma.

And the Spirit of the Dominus came upon him, and he went down to Ascalon, and slew thirty homines of them, and took their spolia, and gave change to them that had given the enigma. And his ira was kindled, and he went up to his pater's domus.

And Samson's uxor was given to his companion, whom he had used as his sodalis.
