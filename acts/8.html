And Saul was consenting unto his death. And at that time there was a great persecution against the ecclesia which was at Jerusalem; and they were all scattered abroad throughout the regions of Judaea and Samaria, except the apostoli.

As for Saul, he made havock of the ecclesia, entering into every domus, and haling men and women committed them to prison.

Therefore they that were scattered abroad went every where praedicare the word.

Then Philip went down to the civitas of Samaria, and praedicared Christum unto them.

And the people with one accord gave heed unto those things which Philip spake, hearing and seeing the signa which he did.

For unclean spiriti, crying with loud voice, came out of many that were possessed with them: and many taken with paralysis, and that were lame, were healed.

And there was great gaudium in that civitas.

But there was a certain man, called Simon, which beforetime in the same civitas used magia, and bewitched the people of Samaria, giving out that himself was some great one:

To whom they all gave heed, from the least to the greatest, saying, This man is the great virtus of God.

And to him they had regard, because that of long time he had bewitched them with magia.

But when they believed Philip praedicans the things concerning the regnum of God, and the nomine of Jesus Christ, they were baptized, both men and women.

Then Simon himself believed also: and when he was baptized, he continued with Philip, and wondered, beholding the signa and great virtutes which were done.

Now when the apostoli which were at Jerusalem heard that Samaria had received the verbum Dei, they sent unto them Petrus and Johannes:

Who, when they were come down, praedixerunt them the Spiritum Sanctum.

For as yet he was fallen upon none of them: only they were baptized in the nomine of the Lord Jesus.

Then laid they their manus upon them, and they received the Spiritum Sanctum.

And when Simon saw that through laying on of the apostolorum manus the Spiritus Sanctus was given, he offered them pecuniam,

Saying, Give me also this potestas, that on whomsoever I lay manus, he may receive the Spiritum Sanctum.

But Petrus said unto him, Thy pecuniam perish with thee, because thou hast thought that the donum of God may be purchased with pecuniam.

Thou hast neither pars nor lot in this matter: for thy cor is not rectus in the conspectu Dei.

Repent therefore of this thy nefaria, and pray God, if perhaps the cogitatio of thy cor may be forgiven thee.

For I perceive that thou art in galla amaritudinis, and in vinculum iniquitatis.

Then answered Simon, and said, Precamini vos pro me to God, that none of these things which ye have spoken come upon me.

And they, when they had testificari and praedicare the verbum of the Lord, returned to Jerusalem, and praedicared the evangelium in many castella of the Samaritanorum.

And the angelus Domini spake unto Philip, saying, Arise, and go toward the meridian, and go along the via that goeth down from Jerusalem unto Gaza, which is desertum.

And he arose and went: and, behold, a man of Ethiopia, an eunuchus of great authority under Candace regina of the Ethiopians, who had the cura of all her thesaurus, and had come to Jerusalem to adore.

And he was returning, and sitting in his currus, he was reading the prophetam Esaiam.

And the Spiritus said unto Philip, Go near, and join thyself to this currus.

And Philip ran thither to him, and heard him read the prophetam Esaiam, and said, Understandest thou what thou readest?

And he said, How can I, except some man guide me? And he desired Philip that he would come up and sit with him.

The place of the Scriptura which he read was this, He was led as a ovicula to the mactatio, and as a agnus before a tonsor, so opened he not his os: In his humiliation his judicium was taken away: and who shall declare his generation? for his vita is taken from the terra.

And the eunuchus answered Philip, and said, I pray thee, of whom speaketh the prophetas this? of himself, or of some other man?

Then Philip opened his os, and began at the same Scriptura, and praedicared unto him Jesus.

And as they went on their via, they came unto a certain aqua: and the eunuchus said, See, here is aqua; what doth hinder me to be baptized?

And Philip said, If thou credis with all thy cor, thou mayest. And he answered and said, I credo that Jesus Christus is the Filius Dei.

And he commanded the currus to stand still: and they went down both into the aqua, both Philip and the eunuchus; and he baptized him.

And when they were come up out of the aqua, the Spiritus Domini caught away Philip, that the eunuchus saw him no more: and he went on his via rejoicing.

But Philip was found at Azotus: and passing through he praedicared in all the civitates, till he came to Caesarea.
