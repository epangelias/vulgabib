Hebrews 3   Back   Next

Wherefore, holy brethren, partakers of the heavenly vocatio, consider the Apostle and High Priest of our confessio, Jesus;

Who was faithful to him that appointed him, as also Moses was faithful in all his domus;

For this man was counted worthy of more gloria than Moses, inasmuch as he who hath built the domus hath more honor than the domus;

For every domus is built by some man; but he that built all things is Deus;

And Moses verily was faithful in all his domus, as a servus, for a testimonium of those things which were to be spoken after;

But Christ as a filius over his own domus; whose domus are we, if we hold fast the confidence and the rejoicing of the hope firm unto the end;

Wherefore, as the Holy Spirit saith, Today if ye will hear his vocem, Harden not your cordia, as in the provocation, in the day of temptation in the wilderness;

When your patres tempted me, proved me, and saw my opera forty years;

Wherefore I was grieved with that generatio, and said, They do alway err in their cor, and they have not known my ways;

So I sware in my ira, They shall not enter into my requiem;

Beware, brethren, lest there be in any of you an evil cor of unbelief, in departing from the Deus vivus;

But exhort one another daily, while it is called To-day; lest any of you be hardened through the deceitfulness of peccatum;

For we are made partakers of Christ, if we hold the beginning of our fiducia stedfast unto the end;

While it is said, To day if ye will hear his vocem, harden not your cordia, as in the provocation.

For some, when they had heard, did provoke: howbeit not all that came out of Aegypt by Moses;

But with whom was he grieved forty years? was it not with them that had sinned, whose cadavera fell in the wilderness?

And to whom sware he that they should not enter into his requiem, but to them that believed not?

So we see that they could not enter in because of unbelief.
