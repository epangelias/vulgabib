1 Samuel 15:1 Then came Samuel to Saul, and Saul said unto him, Benedictus qui venit in nomine Domini, Blessed be thou that comest in the name of the Lord:

2 and now I will send thee to the Amalekites, and thou shalt destroy them, interficere, utterly, and shalt not spare them, parceres, neither shall thy eye spare them, parcere, nor shalt thou have mercy on them, misereri.

3 But Saul and the people spared Agag, and the best of the sheep, and of the oxen, and of the fatlings, and the lambs, and all that was good, and would not utterly destroy them, interficere, but every thing that was vile and refuse, they destroyed, destruere, utterly.

4 Then came the word of the Lord unto Samuel, saying, 5 It repenteth me that I have set up Saul to be king, for he is turned back from following me, and hath not performed my commandments, imperium, And it grieved Samuel, and he cried unto the Lord all night, nocte.

6 And when Samuel rose early to meet Saul in the morning, mane, he was told, saying, Saul came to Carmel, and, behold, he set him up a monument, monumentum, and is gone about, and passed on, and gone down to Gilgal.

7 And Samuel came to Saul, and Saul said unto him, Benedictus qui venit in nomine Domini, Blessed be thou that comest in the name of the Lord, I have performed the commandment of the Lord, imperium, 8 And Samuel said, What meaneth then this bleating of the sheep in mine ears, and the lowing of the oxen which I hear, audire?

9 And Saul said, They have brought them from the Amalekites, for the people spared the best of the sheep and of the oxen, to sacrifice unto the Lord thy God, and the rest we have utterly destroyed, interficere, 10 Then Samuel said unto Saul, I will tell thee what the Lord hath said to me this night, nocte, Say on, said Saul, 11 And Samuel said, When thou wast little in thine own sight, parvus, wast thou not made the head of the tribes of Israel, and the Lord anointed thee king over Israel?

12 And the Lord sent thee on a journey, and said, Go and utterly destroy, interficere, the sinners the Amalekites, and fight against them until they be consumed, consumere, 13 Wherefore then didst thou not obey the voice of the Lord, but didst fly upon the spoil, and didst evil in the sight of the Lord, malum in oculis Domini?

14 And Saul said unto Samuel, I have obeyed the voice of the Lord, and have gone the way which the Lord sent me, and have brought Agag the king of Amalek, and have utterly destroyed, interficere, the Amalekites, 15 But the people took of the spoil, sheep and oxen, the chief of the things which should have been utterly destroyed, interficere, to sacrifice unto the Lord thy God in Gilgal, 16 And Samuel said unto Saul, Stay, and I will tell thee what the Lord hath said to me this night, nocte, And he said unto him, Say on, 17 And Samuel said, When thou wast little in thine own sight, parvus, wast thou not made the head of the tribes of Israel, and the Lord anointed thee king over Israel?

18 And the Lord sent thee on a journey, and said, Go and utterly destroy, interficere, those sinners the Amalekites, and fight against them until they be consumed, consumere, 19 Wherefore then didst thou not obey the voice of the Lord, but didst fly upon the spoil, and didst evil in the sight of the Lord, malum in oculis Domini?

20 And Saul said unto Samuel, Yea, I have obeyed the voice of the Lord, and have gone the way which the Lord sent me, and have brought Agag the king of Amalek, and have utterly destroyed, interficere, the Amalekites, 21 But the people took of the spoil, the sheep and oxen, the chief of the things which should have been utterly destroyed, interficere, to sacrifice unto the Lord thy God in Gilgal, 22 And Samuel said, Hath the Lord as great delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices, holocaustis et victimis, as in obeying the voice of the Lord, Obedire vocem Domini? Behold, to obey is better than sacrifice, and to hearken than the fat of rams, 23 For rebellion is as the sin of witchcraft, rebellio, and stubbornness is as iniquity and idolatry, idolatria, Because thou hast rejected the word of the Lord, he hath also rejected thee from being king, regem, 24 And Saul said unto Samuel, I have sinned, peccavi, for I have transgressed the commandment of the Lord, and thy words, because I feared the people, and obeyed their voice, 25 Now therefore, I pray thee, pardon my sin, ignosce mihi, and turn again with me, that I may worship the Lord, adorare Dominum, 26 And Samuel said unto Saul, I will not return with thee, for thou hast rejected the word of the Lord, and the Lord hath rejected thee from being king over Israel, regem Israel, 27 And as Samuel turned about to go away, he laid hold upon the skirt of his mantle, and it rent, scissum est, 28 And Samuel said unto him, The Lord hath rent, scidit, the kingdom of Israel from thee this day, and hath given it to a neighbour of thine, that is better than thou, 29 And also the Strength of Israel will not lie nor repent, mentiri nec paenitere, for he is not a man, that he should repent, paenitentia, 30 Then he said, I have sinned, peccavi, yet honour me now, I pray thee, before the elders of my people, and before Israel, and turn again with me, that I may worship the Lord thy God, adorare Dominum Deum tuum, 31 So Samuel turned again after Saul, and Saul worshipped the Lord, adoravit Dominum, 32 Then said Samuel, Bring ye hither to me Agag the king of the Amalekites, and Agag came unto him delicately, And Agag said, Surely the bitterness of death is past, amaritudo mortis praeteriit, 33 And Samuel said, As thy sword hath made women childless, so shall thy mother be childless among women, And Samuel hewed Agag in pieces, interfecit Agag, before the Lord in Gilgal, 34 Then Samuel went to Ramah, and Saul went up to his house to Gibeah of Saul, 35 And Samuel came no more to see Saul until the day of his death, usque ad diem mortis suae, nevertheless Samuel mourned for Saul, because the Lord had rent, scidit, him from being king over Israel, regem Israel.
