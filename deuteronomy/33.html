Deuteronomy 33   Back   Next

1 And this is the blessing, wherewith Moses the man of Deus blessed the filii Israel before he died.

2 And he said, Iahveh came from Sinai, and rose up from Seir unto them; he shined forth from mount Paran, and he came with ten thousands of sancti, at his right hand was the ignis of the law.

3 Yea, he loved the populus; all his sancti are in thy manus: and they sat down at thy pedes, every one shall receive of thy verba.

4 Moses commanded us a lex, even the hereditas of the congregation of Iacob.

5 And he was king in Ieshurun, when the caput of the populus were gathered, and the tribes of Israel were assembled.

6 Let Reuben live, and not die; and let not his homines be few.

7 And this is the blessing of Iudah: and he said, Hear, Iahveh, the vox of Iudah, and bring him unto his populus: let his manus be sufficient for him; and be thou an auxilium to him from his enemies.

8 And of Levi he said, Let thy Thummim and thy Urim be with thy sanctus, whom thou provasti at Massah, and with whom thou contendedst at the waters of Meribah;

9 Who said unto his pater and to his mater, I have not seen him; neither did he acknowledge his fratres, nor knew his own filii: for they have observed thy verbum, and kept thy pactum.

10 They shall teach Iacob thy iudicia, and Israel thy lex: they shall put incensum before thy facies, and holocaustum upon thine altare.

11 Bless, Iahveh, his virtus, and accept the opus of his manus: smite through the lumbi of them that rise up against him, and of them that hate him, that they rise not again.

12 And of Beniamin he said, The dilectus of Iahveh shall dwell in securitate by him; and Iahveh shall cover him all the die, and he shall dwell between his umeri.

13 And of Ioseph he said, Blessed of Iahveh be his terra, for the dona of the caelum from above, and for the rores of the abyssus that lieth beneath;

14 And for the fruges of the solis, and for the dona of the lunae,

15 And for the summitates of the montes of the aevum, and for the dona of the colles of the sempiternum:

16 And for the dona of the terra, and the plenitudo thereof, and for the beneplacitum of him that dwelt in the rubus: let it come upon the caput of Ioseph, and upon the vertex of the Nazareus among his fratres.

17 His dignitas is like the primogeniti of his boves, and his cornua are like the cornua of the unicornis: with them he shall cornu the gentes, even to the fines of the terra: and they are the myriades of Ephraim, and they are the milia of Manasse.

18 And of Zabulon he said, Rejoice, Zabulon, in thy going out; and thou, Issachar, in thy tabernacles.

19 They shall call the populus unto the montem; there they shall offer sacrificia of iustitia: for they shall suck of the abundantia of the mari, and of the thesauri hidden in the arena.

20 And of Gad he said, Blessed be he that enlargeth Gad: he dwelleth as a leo, and teareth the membrum of the armum, and the vertex of the caput.

21 And he provided the prima pars for himself, because there, in a partem of the lexgiver, was he seated; and he came with the caput of the populus, he executed the iustitia of Iahveh, and his iudicia with Israel.

22 And of Dan he said, Dan is a lupus solitarius; he shall also rise from Bashan.

23 And of Nephthali he said, O Nephthali, satisfied with favorem, and full of the benedictio of Iahveh: possess thou the mari and the meridies;

24 And of Aser he said, Aser, thy frumentum shall be abundantia, and he shall yield frumentum unto the reges, and thy pedes shall be dipt in oleum.

25 Thy balteus shall be of ferrum, and of aes thy ligamina: and thy fortitudo shall be equal to the dies of thy vita.

26 There is none like unto the Deus Ieshurun, who rideth upon the caelum in thy auxilium, and in his maiestate upon the nubes.

27 The Deus of the aevum is thy refugium, and underneath are the brachia of the sempiternum: and he shall thrust out the inimicum from before thy facies, saying, Disperde.

28 Israel then shall dwell in securitate alone: the fons of Iacob shall be upon a terra of grano and vini; also his caelum shall distill the rores of the oleum.

29 Happy art thou, O Israel: who is like unto thee, O populus saved by Iahveh, the scutum of thy auxilium, and who is the gladius of thy maiestate? and thine inimici shall be found liars unto thee; and thou shalt tread upon their altare.
