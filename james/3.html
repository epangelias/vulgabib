James 3   Back   Next

My brethren, be not many masters, knowing that we receive the greater damnatio, because ye shall receive the greater iudicium.

For in many things we offend all, if any man offend not in word, the same is a perfect homo, able also to bridle the whole corpus.

Behold, we put bits in the mouths of equi, that they may obey us; and we turn about their whole corpus.

Behold also the naucleri, how they guide the great ship, although it is so great, and is driven of fierce venti.

Even so the lingua is a little membrum, and boasteth great things. Behold, how great a materiam a little ignis kindleth!

And the lingua is a ignis, a world of iniquitas: so is the lingua among our membra, that it defileth the whole corpus, and setteth on fire the rotam of our vita, and is set on fire of gehenna.

For every kind of beasts, and of birds, and of serpentes, and of things in the mari, hath been tamed of the humani genus:

But the lingua can no man tame; it is an unruly malum, full of deadly venenum.

Therewith bless we the Deus and Father; and therewith curse we men, which are made after the similitudo of Deus.

Out of the same os proceedeth blessing and cursing. My brethren, these things ought not so to be.

Doth a fons send forth at the same place dulcem and amarum water?

Can the fig tree, my brethren, bear olivas, or a vine, figs? so can no fons both yield dulcem and amarum water.
