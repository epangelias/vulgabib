The Vulgabib

Ruth 3   Back   Next

1. Then Naomi her mother in law said unto her, My filia, shall I not seek rest for thee, that it may be bene with thee?

2. And now is not Boaz of our cognatio, with whose puellae thou wast? Behold, he winnoweth barley to night in the area.

3. Wash thyself therefore, and anoint thee, and put thy vestimenta upon thee, and go down to the area: but make not thyself known to the vir till he shall have consummated to eat and drink.

4. And it shall be, when he lieth down, that thou shalt nota the locus where he shall lie, and thou shalt veni and uncover his pedes, and lay thee down; and he shall te dicere what thou shalt do.

5. And she said unto her, All that thou sayest I will do.

6. And she went down unto the area, and did according to all that her mother in law bade her.

7. And when Boaz had eaten and drunk, and his cor was good, he went to lie down at the extremum of the horreum: and she came softiter, and uncovered his pedes, and laid her down.

8. And it came to pass at midnight, that the vir was terrified, and turned himself, and behold, a mulier lay at his pedes.

9. And he said, Quis es tu? And she answered, I am Ruth thy famula: spread therefore thy vestimenta over thy famula; for thou art a propinquus of mine.

10. And he said, Blessed art thou of the Domini, my filia: thou hast showed more beneficentia in the novissimo than at the principio, in that thou followedst not young viri, whether paucae or multae.

11. And now, my filia, fear not; I will do to thee all that thou requirest: for all the porta of my populus doth know that thou art a mulier of virtus.

12. And now it is vero, that I am thy propinquus: howbeit there is a propinquus nearer than I.

13. Tarry this nocte, and when the mane shall appear, if he will take thee, bene; let him take thee: but if he will not take thee, then will I take thee, as the Domini liveth: lie down until the mane.

14. And she lay at his pedes until the mane: and she rose up before one could nosse another. And he said, Let it not be notum that a mulier came into the horreum.

15. Also he said, Bring the vestimenta that thou hast upon thee, and hold it. And when she held it, he measured six modii of barley, and laid it on her: and she went into the civitas.

16. And when she came to her mother in law, she said, Quis es tu, my filia? And she told her all that the vir had done to her.

17. And she said, These six modii of barley gave he me; for he said to me, Go not empty unto thy mother in law.

18. Then said she, Sit still, my filia, until thou shalt know how the res shall fall: for the vir will not rest, until he shall have finire this res to die.
