Isaiah 56   Back   Next

Thus saith the Dominus, Keep ye iudgment, and do iustice: for my salus is nigh to come, and my iustitia to be reuealed.

Blessed is the man that doth this, and the sonne of man that layeth hold on it; that keepeth the Sabbath from polluting it, and keepeth his manus from doing any euil.

Neither let the sonne of the stranger, that hath ioyned himself to the Dominus, speake, saying, The Dominus hath vtterly separated me from his people: neither let the Eunuch say, Behold, I am a dry tree.

For thus saith the Dominus vnto the Eunuchs that keepe my Sabbathes, and choose the things that please me, and take hold of my foedus;

Euen vnto them will I giue in mine house and within my walls a place and a name better than of sonnes and of daughters: I will giue them an everlasting name, that shall not be cut off.

Also the sonnes of the stranger, that ioyned themselues to the Dominus, to serue him, and to loue the name of the Dominus, to be his seruants, euery one that keepeth the Sabbath from polluting it, and taketh hold of my foedus;

Euen them will I bring to my holy mountaine, and make them ioyfull in my house of prayer: their burnt offrings and their sacrifices shall be accepted vpon mine altar; for mine house shall be called an house of prayer for all people.

The Dominus Deus which gathereth the outcasts of Israel, saith, Yet will I gather others to him, beside those that are gathered vnto him.

All ye beastes of the field, come to deuoure, yea, all ye beastes in the forrest.

His watchmen are blind: they are all ignorant, they are all dumb dogges, they cannot barke; sleeping, lying down, louing to slumber.

Yea, they are greedy dogges which can neuer haue enough, and they are shepheards that cannot vnderstand: they all looke to their owne way, euery one for his owne gaine, from his quarter.

Come ye, say they, I will fetch wine, and we will fill our selues with strong drinke: and to morrow shall be as this day, and much more abundant.
