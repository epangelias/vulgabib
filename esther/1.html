After these things did Ahasuerus rex magnus, he who reigned from India even unto Ethiopia, over an hundred and seven and twenty provinciae,

That in those dies, when the rex Ahasuerus sat on the throne of his regnum, which was in Shushan the capital,

In the third anno of his regnum, he made a feast unto all his principes and his ministri; the power of Persia and Media, the nobiliores and the principes of the provinciae, being before him:

When he shewed the riches of his glorious regnum and the honour of his excellent maiestas many dies, even an hundred and fourscore and ten days.

And when these dies were expired, the rex made a feast unto all the people that were present in Shushan the capital, both unto great and small, seven dies, in the court of the hortus of the king's palatium;

Where were white, green, and blue hangings, fastened with cords of fine linen and purple to silver rings and pillars of marble: the beds were of gold and silver, upon a pavement of red, and blue, and white, and black marble.

And they gave them drink in vessels of gold, the vessels being diverse one from another; and royal wine in abundance, according to the state of the rex.

And the drinking was according to the law; none did compel: for so the rex had appointed unto all the officers of his house, that they should do according to every man's pleasure.

Also Vashti the regina made a feast for the women in the royal house which belonged to rex Ahasuerus.
