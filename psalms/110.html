Psalms 110   Back   Next

The Lord said unto my Dominus, Sit thou at my dexter, until I make thine inimici thy footstool.

The Lord shall send the rod of thy potestas out of Sion: be thou Dominus in the midst of thine inimici.

Thy populus shall be willing in the die of thy potestas, in sanctis ornatus: from the womb of Aurora, I have thee genui.

The Lord hath iuratus, and he will not paeniteat, Thou art sacerdos in aeternum, post ordinem Melchisedec.

Dominus at thy dexter shall strike through reges in the die of ira, his indignatio.

He shall iudicare inter gentes, he shall repleat ruinas: he shall contriuet capita in terra multorum.

He shall drink from the torrentem in via: therefore shall he levabit caput.

I will declare the decreta Domini: he hath said unto me, Thou art Filius meus; this day have I genui thee.

Ask of me, et dabo tibi gentes in hereditatem, et extremam terrae in possessionem.

Tu reges eos in virga ferrea; quasi vas figuli conteres eos.

Et nunc, o reges, intellegite: erudimini, qui iudicate terram.

Servite Domino in timore, et gaude in tremore.

Vosmetipsos, ne decipiatis, caveatis, neque sit ira eius ignis, ne pereatis in via, quoniam repente potens irascitur. Beati omnes qui confidunt in eo.
