The Vulgabib

Psalms 124   Back   Next

1 If that I forget thee, Ierusalem, let my right manus oblivioni tradatur.

2 Let my lingua cleave to the roof of my os, if I do not remember thee, if I do not prefer Ierusalem above my caput gaudii.

3 Let the filii Edom say, Who will usque ad fundus lead us, and who will deducere nos in Ierusalem?

4 But Ierusalem, which is built as a civitas, that is compacta together:

5 For thither the gentes shall ascend, the gentes of the Dei, to confess the nomen Domini in Ierusalem.

6 For there are sediae set for iudicium, sediae of the domus David.

7 Pray that pace may be within thy muri, and prosperitas within thy turres.

8 For my fratres and amici, I will dicere, Pace sit in te.

9 Because of the domus Domini Dei nostri, I will require bonum for thee, Ierusalem.
