Ecclesiastes 12   Back   Next

Remember now thy Creator in the dies of thy iuventus, while the anni of thy life are not yet filled with malum.

While the sol is not darkened, nor the luna, nor the stellae, nor the nubes returne after the pluvia:

In the die when the custodes of the domus shall tremble, and the fortes viri shall bow themselves, and the molares shall cease, because they are few, and the viae of the vision shall be obscurae, and the flores of the amygdalae shall be cut off, and the desiderium of the animus shall cease, and the mercator shall not go any more per viam:

In the die when they shall fear the altum, and the viae shall be obscurae, and the flores of the amygdalae shall be cut off, and the desiderium of the animus shall cease, and the mercator shall not go any more per viam:

Or ever the pulvis returne to the terra, as it was, and the spiritus returne to Deus who gave it.

Vanitas vanitatum, dixit Ecclesiastes, vanitas vanitatum, omnia vanitas.

Et finis sermonis omnis auditus: Timor Dei, et mandata eius observare, hoc est omnis homo.

Quia Deus iudicabit omnia opera, etiam omne occultum, sive bonum sive malum.

Et tamen, si quisquam expertus fuerit aliqua disciplina, sciat se non habere certam spem vitae, sed in omnibus suis operibus invenietur incertus.

Et ideo, quia omnia sub sole vanitas, et labor et dolor, et quia omnia transitoria, et quia nihil certi habemus in vita nostra, nisi peccatum et mortem.
