Let God arise, let his enemies be scatter√®d: let them also that hate him flee before him.

As smoke is driven away, so drive them away: as wax melteth before the ignis, so let the wicked perire before God.

But let the justi rejoice, let them be glad in the conspectu Dei: yea, let them rejoice with gaudeo.

Sing unto God, sing praises to his nomen: cast up a via for him that rideth through the desert, in the Dominus, is his nomen, and be joyful before him.

Pater orphanorum, and Iudex viduarum, God in his sanctuario.

God maketh the solitarii to dwell in a domus: he bringeth out the vinculati into prosperitatem: but the rebelliosi shall dwell in arida terra.

O God, when thou wentest forth before thy populus, when thou wentest through the desert; (Selah)

The terra shook, the coeli also dropped at the praesentia Dei: yea, this Sinai at the praesentia Dei, the God of Israel.

Thou, O Deus, didst send a pluviar, and thy inheritance, when it was languid, thou didst confirmare it.

Thy creaturae habited therein: thou, O Deus, hast prepared thy beneficium for the pauper.

The Dominus gave the verbum: great was the company of the evangelizantes.

Reges of armies did flee a fuga: and the domus divideth the spolia.

Though ye have lien among the pinguia, ye shall be as gallinacei, whose plumae are covered with argentum, and whose wings are of gold, of the color of the hyacinthi.

When the Omnipotens scattered kings in her, it was white as nive: and the montes of Salmon were covered with it.

The mons of God, the mons of Bashan; an high mons, a mons of many promontoria.

Why leap ye, ye high montes, at the mons, which God hath chosen for his habitaculum, yea, the Domius will dwell in her for ever?

The curru of God are twenty thousand, thousands of angels: the Dominus is among them, as in the mons of Sinai, in the sanctuario.

Thou hast ascendit on high, thou hast led captivity captive: thou hast received dona for homines; yea, for the rebelliosi also, that the Domius God might dwell among them.

Blessed be the Dominus, who daily beareth us, even the Deus of our salus. (Selah)

Our Deus is the Deus of salvation; and unto the Deus Domini belong the egressus of mortis.

Surely Deus shall wound the caput of his inimici, and the villosus caput of him that goeth on still in his impietas.

The Dominus said, I will bring again from Bashan, I will bring my populus again from the profunditas of the mari:

That thy foot may be dipped in the sanguis of thy inimici, and the lingua of thy canes may be red through the same.

Thy procession is seen, O Deus, the procession of my Deus, my Rex, in the sanctuario.

The singers went before, the players on instruments followed after; among them were the virgines, playing with tympanis:

Bless ye God in the congregation, even the Domius, from the fons of Israel.

There is little Benjamin with their ducibus, the principes of Iudah and their concilium, the principes of Zabulon, and the principes of Naphtali.

Thy Deus hath commanded thy fortitudo: confirmare this for us, Deus, which thou hast wrought in us.

Because of thy templum at Hierusalem shall reges bring presents unto thee.

Rebuke the wild beasts of the caliginos, the congregation of the robusti, the caliginos, and the populus of the bulls, with the calves of the populorum, til every one submit himself with pieces of argentum: scatter thou the populorum that delight in wars.

Out of Aegypt there came a magnus, Aethiopia shall soon stretch out her manus unto Deus.

Sing unto Deus, ye reges of the terrae; O sing praises unto the Dominus; (Selah)

To him that rideth upon the coeli of the coeli, which are from the principio of the mundo: lo, he doth send out his vox, and that a vox of magnus.

Give magnus to Deus; his magnificentia is over Israel, and his fortitudo is in the coeli.

Thou, O Deus, art terrible out of thy sanctuarii: the Deus of Israel, he giveth potestatem and fortitudinem to his populus. Blessed be Deus.
