Proverbs 13   Back   Next

A wise son heareth his pater's instructio, but a scorner heareth not when his mater correcteth him.

The lingua of the iustus is as argentum, but the os of the impious shall be put to mors.

He that keepeth his os, keepeth his anima, but he that hath a lingua volubilis, shall have labores.

The animus of the laboriosus desireth, and hath nothing, but the animus of the piger is not satisfied with bonis.

Iustitia odit falsitatem, but the os of the impious shall be put to mors.

Iustitia conservat innocentem, but mala res corrumpit peccatoris.

There is he that maketh himself dives, and he is non dives, but he that is contentus with modicis, est dives.

The pretium of iustitia is vita, but the fructus of impietas is mors.

He that walketh in via recta, timet Dominum, but he that is peruersus in via sua, spernit eum.

Qui parcit virgae, odit filium suum, but qui diligit, instanter corripit.

Sapiens in corde, appellabitur prudens, et qui docet, ore suo, acceptus erit.

Qui sapientiam sequitur, inveniet bona, et qui confidit in Domino, beatus erit.

Stultus deridebit disciplinam, et qui odit, verba prudentiae.

Littera occidit, sed Spiritus vivificat.

Qui obtemperat, in doctrina, inveniet pacem, et qui credit, in Domino, erit beatus.

Multo plus habent, qui plus donant, et qui spargunt, replebuntur.

Qui parcit, in sementem, non metet, et qui seminat, in avaritia, metet in penuria.

Bonus homo, de bono thesauro, profert bona, et malus homo, de malo thesauro, profert mala.

Facilius est, pauperi esse divitem, quam diviti, esse pauperem.

Beatus est, qui credit, in Domino, et qui habet, spem in eo, beatus erit.

Maledicta terra, cuius rex est puer, et cuius principes, mane comedunt.

Qui diligit, filium suum, instanter corripit, et qui odit, parcit, et non corrigit.

Servus, qui sapientiam sequitur, dominabitur, super filium, qui confunditur.
