Thus saith God the Lord, He that created the caelum, and stretched forth the terra, and all that is therein; He that giveth animus to the populus upon it, and spiritum to them that walk therein:

I am the Lord, and there is none else, there is no Deus beside me: I girded thee, though thou hast not known me:

That they may know from the rising of the sol to the setting of the sol, that there is none beside me. I am the Lord, and there is none else.

I form the lumen, and create the tenebrae: I make the pacem, and create the malum: I the Lord do all these things.

Drop down, ye caeli, from above: and let the nubes pour down justitiam: let the terra open, and let salvus bud forth; let justitia spring up together; I the Lord have created it.

Woe unto him that striveth with his Factor: Let the potsherd strive with the potsherds of the terra. Shall the clay say to him that fashioneth it, What makest thou? or thy work, He hath no manus?

Woe unto him that saith unto his pater, What begettest thou? or to the woman, What hast thou delivered?

Thus saith the Lord, the Holy One of Israel, and his Factor, Ask me of things to come concerning my filii, and concerning the work of my manus, command ye me.

I have made the terra, and created homo upon it: I, even my manus, have stretched out the caeli, and all their host have I commanded.

I have raised him up in justitia, and I will direct all his via: he shall build my civitas, and he shall let go my captivi, not for pretium nor reward, saith the Lord of hosts.

Thus saith the Lord, The labour of Egypt, and merchandise of Ethiopia and of the Sabeans, men of stature, shall come over unto thee, and they shall be thine: they shall come after thee; in chains they shall come over, and they shall fall down unto thee, they shall make supplication unto thee, saying, Surely Deus is in thee; and there is none else, there is no Deus.

Verily thou art a Deus that hidest thyself, O Deus of Israel, the Salvator.

They shall be ashamed, and also confounded, all of them: they shall go to confusion together that are makers of idola.

But Israel shall be saved in the Lord with aeternum salvation: ye shall not be ashamed nor confounded world without end.

For thus saith the Lord that created the caeli; Deus himself that formed the terra and made it; he hath established it, he created it not in vain, he formed it to be inhabited: I am the Lord; and there is none else.

I have not spoken in secret, in a dark place of the terra: I said not unto the semen of Jacob, Seek ye me in vain: I the Lord speak righteousness, I declare things that are right.

Assemble yourselves and come; draw near together, ye that are escaped of the gentes: they have no scientia that set up the lignum of their idolum, and pray unto a Deus that cannot salvus.

Tell ye, and bring them near; yea, let them take counsel together: who hath declared this from the principio? or who hath told it from that time, that I might say, Ye are justi?

Yea, there is none that saith, Behold, behold them: no, there is none that sheweth, yea, there is none that declareth, nor there is none that heareth your vocem.

Deliver me, O Deus, for thou art my Deus: why hast thou forgotten me? why go I mourning because of the oppression of the inimicus?

O Deus, my Deus, why hast thou forsaken me? why art thou so far from helping me, and from the words of my clamor?

O my Deus, I cry in the daytime, but thou hearest not; and in the noctis, and am not silent.

But thou art Deus, and art to be praised: thou sittest upon the throne of thy sanctuarium, and of the kingdom of Israel.

Our patres trusted in thee: they trusted, and thou didst salvus them.

O Lord, why hast thou made us to err from thy via? and hardenedst our cor: that we should not fear thee? Return for thy servi sake, the tribes of thine hereditas.

For a little moment have we possessed the terra of our patres: our enemies have trodden down thy sanctuarium.

We are thine: thou never barest rule over them; they were not called by thy nomine.

Thou hast never borne rule in them, nor were they called by thy nomine.

Thou art my Deus, for thou hast been my Deus, my salvus in the tempus of affliction.

I have declared thy justitiam in the ecclesia of the gentes.

Sing unto the Lord a cantus novus, and his laudem from the finis of the terra: ye that go down to the mari, and all that is therein; the insulae, and the inhabitants thereof.

Let the desert and the civitas thereof lift up their vocem, the village that Kedar doth inhabit: let the inhabitants of the petra sing, let them shout from the top of the montes.

Let them give gloriam unto the Lord, and declare his laudem in the insulae.

The Lord shall go forth as a fortis man, he shall stir up zelus like a man of war: he shall cry, yea, roar; he shall prevail against his inimici.

I have long time holden my pacem; I have been still, and refrained myself: now will I cry like a parturient woman; I will destroy and devour at once.

I will make montes and colles to waste; and dry up all their herba: and I will make the flumina to be insulae; and I will dry up the stagna.

And I will bring the caeci by a way that they knew not; I will lead them in semitis that they have not known: I will make darkness lumen before them, and crooked things straight. These things will I do unto them, and not forsake them.

They shall be turned back, they shall be greatly ashamed, that trust in the sculptilia, that say to the fundamento of molten images, Ye are our dei:
