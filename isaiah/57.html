The Vulgabib

Isaiah 57   Back   Next

1. Righteous ones perisheth, and no homo comprehendeth: and merciful men are taketh away, neque is anyone who understandeth that the righteous are taketh away from malum.

2. He entereth into pacem: they rest in their beds, each one walking in his rectus via.

3. But thou, veni, approach hither, ye filii of the sorceress, seminis of the adulterer and the meretrix.

4. Against whom do you jest? Against whom maketh thou a wide os, and prolongas the linguam? Are ye not filii of transgression, a false seed?

5. Inflamming yourselves with idolis under every green arborem, thou sacrificas children in the brooklets, under the clefts of the petrae.

6. In the partes of the brook is thy pars: they, they are thy sorte: and to them thou hast effusus a libation, thou hast offerebas a oblation.

7. Upon a monticello of high elevation, and on a collis thou hast posited thy bed: and thou ascendest up thither to sacrificare.

8. And behind the ostium, and the postis thou hast posited thy memoria: for thou hast reveled against me, and hast multiplicatus thy concubitus.

9. And thou hast trusted in regem with multus unguentum, and hast multiplicatus thy ointments, and hast sent nuntios into Afar, even to Sheol itself.

10. Thou art wearied in the multitudinem of thy vias; yet thou sayest not, 'There is requies': thou hast found vita in thy via, and therefore thou wilt not desist.

11. And for whom hast thou been afraid or feared, that thou hast mentitus, and hast not remembered me, nor taken me in cor?

12. I will declare thy justitia, and thy opera: and they shall not profit thee.

13. When thou criest, let them deliver thee, whom thou hast multiplicatus: let them liberate thee in the tempus of afflictio: for multus is thy numero.

14. And thus dico to those who confidunt in me, Behold, Salvus will be thine. And thou shalt say, Destroy, O Domine, and separate them that afflict me, and draw nigh, and salvare my animus.

15. For thus dicit the Altissimus, Qui habitat in aeternum, Sanctus Nomen ejus, Who dwelleth in sanctuario, in altitudinem and sanctuario dicit: Qui docet quiescere in quietem, and dat munus et quies and sperare in Spe.

16. For I will not contend for ever, neither will I be iratus alway: for spiritus meus and Anima mea with infirmi and solum sufferentes and despiciens and tolerans usque in finem.

17. For the iniquitas of his cupiditas, I was iratus and striked him, and I hid me and was iratus: and he went after cupiditas sui animi.

18. I have seen his vias, and I will sana him, and I will conducere him and restore consolations to him and to those who lugent him.

19. Creating the fructus of the labia: Pacem, pacem, ad longe, et ad proxime, dicit Domine, and Sana all malum.

20. But the impii are similis turbulent sea, qui non potest quiescere, and ejus aquae spumantia sicut cenum et lutum.

21. Non est pax, dicit Dominus Deus meus, impiis.
