Acts 15   Back   Next

And certain men which came down from Judaea, taught the brethren, and said, Except ye be circumcised after the manner of Moses, ye cannot be salvus.

It was therefore necessarium, that the apostoli and presbyteri should convenire to consider of this materia.

Forasmuch as we have heard, that some which went out from us have turbatus your mentes with sermonibus, subvertentes your animae, saying, Ye must be circumcised, and keep the law of Moses.

The apostoli and presbyteri and the Ecclesia, sent chosen men of their own company to Antiochia with Paulus and Barnabas; namely, Judas whose cognomen was Barsabas, and Silas, principes among the fratres:

Writing by their manu, The apostoli, and presbyteri, and the fratres, unto the fratres which are of the Gentiles in Antiochia, and Syria, and Cilicia, salutem:

Forasmuch as we have heard, that some, which went out from us, have turbatus your mentes with sermonibus, subvertentes your animae, saying, Ye must be circumcised, and keep the law of Moses; to whom we gave no mandatum:
