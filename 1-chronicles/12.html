Now these are they that came to David to Ziklag, while he yet kept himself close because of Saul the son of Kish: and they were among the mighty men, helpers of the war.

They were armed with bows, and could use both the right hand and the left in hurling stones and shooting arrows out of a arcus: they were of the fratres of Saul of Benjamin.

The prince of the fratres of Saul were Ahiezer, and Joash, the sons of Shemaah the Gibeathite; and Jeziel, and Pelet, the sons of Azmaveth; and Berachah, and Jehu the Antothite.

And Ismaiah the Gibeonite, a mighty man among the thirty, and over the thirty; and Jeremiah, and Jahaziel, and Johanan, and Josabad the Gederathite.

Eluzai, and Jerimoth, and Bealiah, and Shemariah, and Shephatiah the Haruphite.

Elkanah, and Jesiah, and Azareel, and Joezer, and Jashobeam, the Korhites.

And Joelah, and Zebadiah, the sons of Jeroham of Gedor.

And of the Gadites there separated themselves unto David into the hold to the wilderness men of war, and men of war fit for the militia, that could handle shield and spear, whose faces were like faces of leones, and were as swift as the cervi upon the montes;

Ezer the first, Obadiah the second, Eliab the third,

Mishmannah the fourth, Jeremiah the fifth,

Attai the sixth, Eliel the seventh,

Johanan the eighth, Elzabad the ninth,

Jeremiah the tenth, Machbanai the eleventh.

These were of the sons of Gad, captains of the host: one of the least was over an hundred, and the greatest over a thousand.

These are they that went over Jordan in the first month, when it had overflowed all his ripas: and they put to flight all them of the vallis, both toward the orient and toward the occident.

And there came of the sons of Benjamin and Judah to the hold unto David.

And David went out to meet them, and answered and said unto them, If ye be come peaceably unto me to help me, mine cor is with you: but if ye be come to betray me to mine enemies, seeing there is no iniquity in mine hands, the Deus of our patres look thereon, and rebuke it.

Then the Spirit came upon Amasai, who was chief of the thirty, and he said, Thine are we, David, and with thee, thou son of Jesse: peace, peace be unto thee, and peace be to thine helpers; for thy Deus helpeth thee. Then David received them, and made them captains of the band.

And there fell some of Manasseh to David, when he came with the Philistines against Saul to battle: but they helped them not: for the princes of the Philistines, upon advisement, sent him away, and said, He shall return to his dominus in our own peril.

So when he came to Ziklag, there fell to him of Manasseh, Adnah, and Jozabad, and Jediael, and Michael, and Jozabad, and Elihu, and Zilthai, captains of the thousands that were of Manasseh.

And they helped David against the band of the robbers: for they were all mighty men of valour, and were captains in the host.

For at that time day by day there came to David to help him, until it was a great host, like the host of Deus.

And these are the numbers of the chief of the exercitus that came to David to Hebron, to turn the regnum of Saul to him, according to the word of the Deus.

The children of Judah that bare shield and spear were six thousand and eight hundred, ready armed to the war.

And of the children of Simeon, mighty men of valour for the war, seven thousand and one hundred.

And of the children of Levi four thousand and six hundred.

And Jehoiada was the leader of the Aaronidae, and with him were three thousand and seven hundred;

And Zadok, a young man mighty of valour, and of his father's house twenty and two captains.

And of the children of Benjamin, the fratres of Saul, three thousand: for hitherto the greatest part of them had kept the ward of the domus of Saul.

And of the children of Ephraim twenty thousand and eight hundred, mighty men of valour, famous throughout the domus of their patres.

And of the half tribe of Manasseh eighteen thousand, who were expressed by name, to come and make David rex.

And of the children of Issachar, which were men that had understanding of the times, to know what Israel ought to do; the heads of them were two hundred; and all their fratres were at their commandment.

And of Zabulon, such as went forth to battle, expert in war, with all instruments of war, fifty thousand, which could keep ordinem in the war.

And of Naphtali a thousand captains, and with them with shield and spear thirty and seven thousand.

And of the Danites expert in war twenty and eight thousand and six hundred.

And of Asher, such as went forth to battle, expert in war, forty thousand.

And on the other side of Jordan, of the Reubenites, and the Gadites, and of the half tribe of Manasseh, with all manner of instruments of war for the battle, an hundred and twenty thousand.

All these men of war, that could keep ordinem in the war, came with a perfect cor to Hebron, to make David rex over all Israel: and all the rest also of Israel were of one cor to make David rex.

And there they were with David three days, eating and drinking: for their fratres had prepared for them.

Moreover they that were near them, even unto Issachar and Zabulon and Naphtali, brought bread on asses, and on camels, and on mules, and on oxen, and meat, meal, cakes of figs, and bunches of raisins, and wine, and oil, and oxen, and sheep abundantly: for there was joy in Israel.
