Job 28   Back   Next

Surely there is a mine for argentum, and a place where aurum is refined.

Iron is taken out of terra, and brasa is molten out of lapidem.

He setteth flumen in orbem terre, and the lapides of the valley are molten at the face of aer.

He divideth rivum in orbem, and the fontes lapidum are discovered by him.

The terra from which the panis of the metallum is, is gotten in loco nigrum as carbonem.

The lapides of it are the locus of sapphirus, and it hath the aurum of the metallum.

There is a semita, which avis non novit, and which the oculus of vultur non videt:

The pes of lea non pressit, and the filius of ferrea non ambulavit.

He putteth manum suam in petram, and overturneth montem by the radices.

He cutteth rivum in petras, and his oculus seeth omne pretiosum.

He bindeth flumen, that they may not fluere, and the occultum things of the metallum he bringeth forth to lux.

Whence then cometh sapientia, and where is the locus of intellectus?

Notus est, it is not in terra, nec in aqua, nec in aer,

The profundum dicit, Non est in me: et the mare dicit, Non est in me.

Non dabit pro ea aurum, nec appendet argentum in commutatione ejus.

The pretiosum gemma of it, and the sapphirus, non sunt aestimati, nec the aurum, in comparatione ejus.

The corallium, aut the crystal, non sunt, nec the aurum, in comparatione ejus.

Whence then cometh sapientia, where is the locus of intellectus?

Videt eam the oculus, qui vivit in aeternum, qui creavit omnes fines terrae:

Ipse novit locum ejus, et ponit manum suam in eam.

Cum ipse finxerit ventum, et aquas mensurasset in mensura, et viam tempestatum in statera ponderasset:

Tunc ostendit homini sapientiam, et ostendit ei intellectum Dei.

Ecce, timor Domini, ipsa est sapientia, et recedere a malo, intellectus.
