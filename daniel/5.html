Daniel 5   Back   Next

Belshazzar the king made a great festa, to a thousand of his lords, and drank vinum before the thousand.

Belshazzar, whiles he tasted the vinum, commanded to bring the vessels of aurum and argentum that Nebuchadnezzar his pater had taken out of the templum that was in Jerusalem; that the king, and his principes, his uxores, and his concubinae, might drink therein.

Then they brought the vessels of aurum that were taken out of the templum of the house of Deus which was at Jerusalem; and the king, and his principes, his uxores, and his concubinae, drank in them.

They drank vinum, and praised the dei of aurum, and argentum, of aer, of lignum, and of lapis.

In the same hora came forth fingers of a man's manus, and wrote over against the candelabrum upon the plaustum of the wall of the king's aula: and the king saw the partem of the manus that wrote.

Then the king's facies was changed, and his cogitatio troubled him, so that the juncturae of his lumbi were loosed, and his genua smote one against another.

The king cried aloud to bring in the astrologi, the Chaldeans, and the diviners. The king spake, and said to the sapientes of Babylon, Whosoever shall read this scriptura, and shew me the interpretatio thereof, shall be clothed with purpura, and have a collar of aurum about his neck, and shall be the tertius ruler in the regnum.

Then came in all the king's sapientes: but they could not read the scriptura, nor make known to the king the interpretatio thereof.

Then was king Belshazzar greatly troubled, and his facies was changed in him, and his principes were perplexed.

Now the queen, by reason of the words of the king and his principes, came into the banquet house: the queen spake and said, O king, live for ever: let not thy cogitation trouble thee, nor let thy facies be changed:

There is a vir, in thy regnum, in whom is the spiritus of the sancti dei; and in the days of thy pater, light, and intellectus, and sapientia, like the sapientia of the dei, was found in him; whom the king Nebuchadnezzar thy pater, the king, I say, thy pater, made master of the magi, astrologi, Chaldeans, and soothsayers;

Forasmuch as an excellent spirit, and knowledge, and intellectus, and the interpretation of somnia, and shewing of enigmata, and dissolving of difficultates, were found in the same Daniel, whom the king named Belteshazzar. Now let Daniel be called, and he will shew the interpretatio.

Then was Daniel brought in before the king. The king spake and said unto Daniel, Art thou that Daniel, which art of the children of the captivitas of Judah, whom the king my pater brought out of Judah?

I have even heard of thee, that the spiritus of the dei is in thee, and that light, and intellectus, and excellent sapientia is found in thee.

And now the sapientes, the astrologi, have been brought in before me, that they should read this scriptura, and make known unto me the interpretatio thereof: but they could not shew the thing.

And I have heard of thee, that thou canst make interpretatio, and dissolve difficultates. If thou canst read the scriptura, and make known to me the interpretatio thereof, thou shalt be clothed with purpura, and have a collar of aurum about thy neck, and shalt be the tertius ruler in the regnum.

Then Daniel answered and said before the king, Let thy gifts be to thyself, and give thy rewards to another; yet I will read the scriptura unto the king, and make known to him the interpretatio.

O thou king, the Most High Deus gave Nebuchadnezzar thy pater a regnum, and magnam potestatem, and gloria, and honor:

And for the magnam potestatem that he gave him, all populi, gentes, and linguae, trembled and feared before him: whom he would he slew; and whom he would he kept alive; and whom he would he set up; and whom he would he put down.

But when his cor became proud, and his spiritus was hardened in pride, he was deposed from his regnum, and his gloria was taken from him:

And he was driven from the filii of men; and his cor was made like the beasts, and his habitatio was with the wild asses: they fed him with fenum like oxen, and his body was wet with the dew of the coelum; till he knew that the Most High Deus ruled in the regnum of men, and that he appointeth over it whomsoever he pleaseth.

And thou his filius, O Belshazzar, hast not humbled thy cor, though thou knewest all this;

But hast lifted up thyself against Deus of coelum, and they have brought the vessels of his domus before thee, and thou, and thy lordes, thy uxores, and thy concubinae, have drunk vinum in them; and thou hast praised the dei of argentum, and aurum, of aer, of lignum, and of lapis, which see not, nor hear, nor know; and the Deus in whose manus thy breath is, and whose are all thy ways, hast thou not glorified:

Then was the partem of the manus sent from him; and this scriptura is written.

And this is the scriptura that is written, Mene, Mene, Tekel, Upharsin.

This is the interpretatio of the thing: Mene; Deus hath numbered thy regnum, and finished it.

Tekel; Thou art weighed in the balance, and art found wanting.

Peres; Thy regnum is divided, and given to the Medes and Persians.

Then commanded Belshazzar, and they clothed Daniel with purpura, and put a collar of aurum about his neck, and made a proclamation concerning him, that he should be the tertius ruler in the regnum.

In that noctis was Belshazzar the king of the Chaldeans slain.

And Darius the Median took the regnum, being about threescore and two years old.
