Isaiah 52   Back   Next

Awake, awake; put on thy virtus, O Zion; put on thy vestimentum ornatus, O Jerusalem, the holy civitas: for henceforth there shall no more come into thee the incircumcisus and the pollutus.

The Vulgar shall not see thee; the son of the foreigner shall not enter into thee.

Shake thyself from the pulvis, arise, and sit down, O Jerusalem: loose thyself from the vincula of thy cervix, O captive daughter of Zion.

For thus saith Dominus, Ye shall be soldi sine argentum, and ye shall be redempti sine pecunia.

For thus saith Dominus Deus, My Spiritu Sancto, which I put upon thee, and my verbum, which I put in thy os, shall not depart from thy os, nor from the os of thy semen, saith Dominus Deus, from henceforth and for ever.

Awake, awake, stand up, O Jerusalem, which hast bibis potum de manu Domini calicem irae ejus, and hast drunk the potus, and hast exhausted the potus.

There is non eructans, there is non confortans, among all the filii whom thou hast nutriti, there is non taking hold of the manus among all the filii whom thou hast educati.

Thy principes were errantes, and thy pedagogi were rebellantes; and the oculus of the videbit was covered, that they should not vide, and the cor of the intelligere was covered, that they should not intelligere.

The Prophetia of Isaia, filius Amos, which he vidit super Judah and Jerusalem in diebus Uzziae, Joathan, Ahaz, and Ezekiae, regibus Judae.

The Lord hath mingled a vinum fallacia in medio eorum, and they eructabant; and omnes erraverunt in via sua, every pedagogus in his scelere, and every pastor in his avoritia.

And they eructabant in plasmare dominorum suorum, saying, He calore et ebrietate linguae; this people deridebat the prophetae, and contemnebat the sermocinatio of the sancti.

For this, my indignatio shall not quiescere, but in flamma ignis exardebit; and my populus in labiis ignis shall comedere, and my creaturae in igne succendere.

Ye looked for lucem, et ecce tenebrae; in lumine, et ecce caligo; they shall derelinquere te cum calce, and errabunt in tenebris; et omnes pedagogi errabunt in labiryntho; and there shall be clamor magnum super eos.

For it shall be sicut incendium ardens in igne, quod vorabit in se ipso, et in caligine; et erit sicut terrae frumenta aruerint in facie solis, et flos eius effloruit ante florem, et germinaverunt, et egressa est in spicam, et infirmata est, et in sicco et caliginoso vento exaruit, et succensa est igni; sic et erit multitudo populi mei; et derelinquetur quasi quercus post decorem folii, et sicut oliva arida post collectionem fructus, dicit Dominus Deus.

The Domini shall give vocem; for his exercitus multus, and his fortitudo in exercitu suo, and in suavitatem sui imperii; and he dabit vocem in die belli, et conteretur in operibus suis, et derelinquetur quasi quercus post decorem folii, et sicut oliva arida post collectionem fructus, dicit Dominus Deus.
