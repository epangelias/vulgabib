Deuteronomy 24   Back   Next

When a man hath taken a nova sponsa, he shall not go out to war, neither shall he be charged with any business: but he shall be free at domus sua one year, and shall rejoice with his nova sponsa which he hath taken.

No man shall take the superior and inferior molare of an animal in pledge, neque shall he take the inferior molare of an animal that is used for any other purpose.

If a man rise up by nocte, and strike his proximus, that he die; and he that smote him shall be culpabilis of sanguis.

If a man deliver unto his proximus an asinus, or an ox, or a sheep, or any beast to keep; and it die, or be wounded, or driven away, no man seeing it:

Then shall an oath of the Domini be between them both, that he hath not put his manus unto his proximus' res: and the owner of it shall accept thereof, and he shall not make it bonum.

If it be stolen from him, he shall make restitution unto the owner thereof.

If the fur be stolen from his domus, he shall make restitution for the fur in the presence of the Domini.

If a man deliver unto his proximus raiment to keep, and it be stolen out of the hominis domus, if the fur be stolen, he shall make restitution.

If the fur be not stolen, but be found, then let them come unto the judices, that it may be judged whether he that hath it in custodia, or the owner of it, hath put his manus unto his proximus' res.

In every causa of trespass, whether it be for ox, or for asinus, or sheep, or raiment, or any manner of lost thing which another challengeth to be his, the causa of both parties shall come before the judices; and whom the judices shall condemn, he shall pay double unto his proximus.

If a man deliver unto his proximus an asinus, or an ox, or a sheep, or any beast to keep; and it die, or be wounded, or driven away, seeing it:

He shall pay the damnum of it, and shall restore it.

And if a man borrow ought of his proximus, and it be hurt, or die, the owner thereof being not with it, he shall surely make it bonum.

But if the owner thereof were with it, he should not make it bonum: if it were an hired thing, it came for his merces.

If a man entice a virgo that is not desponsata, and lie with her, he shall endote her to him, to be his uxor.

If her pater utterly refuse to give her unto him, he shall pay pecuniam according to the dotem of virgines.

Thou shalt not suffer a sortilegus to live.

Whoever lieth with a bestiam shall surely be put to death.

He that sacrificeth unto any deus, save unto the Domini only, he shall be put to death.

Thou shalt not vex a peregrinus, neither shalt thou oppress him: for ye were peregrini in terra Aegypti.

Thou shalt not pervert the judicium of the peregrinus, nor of the pupillus, nor of the vidua, neither shalt thou take a vestimentum of a vidua to pledge:

But thou shalt remember that thou wast a servus in terra Aegypti, and the Domini thy Deus redeemed thee thence: therefore I command thee to do this thing.

When thou cuttest down thy segetem, and hast forgot a manipulum in the ager, thou shalt not go again to fetch it: it shall be for the peregrinus, for the pupillus, and for the vidua: that the Domini thy Deus may bless thee in all the opera of thine manus which thou doest.

When thou beatest thine olivetum, thou shalt not go over the branches again: it shall be for the peregrinus, for the pupillus, and for the vidua.

When thou gatherest the vinum of thy vinea, thou shalt not glean it afterward: it shall be for the peregrinus, for the pupillus, and for the vidua.

And thou shalt remember that thou wast a servus in terra Aegypti: therefore I command thee to do this thing.
