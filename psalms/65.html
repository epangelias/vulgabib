To the chief Musician, A Psalm and Song of David. Deus, Deus meus, erigo mane, et expecto te, because thou hast been my help, and in the shadow of thy wings will I rejoice.

My voice shalt thou hear in the morning, O Lord; in the morning will I direct my prayer unto thee, and will look up.

For thou art not a Deus of voluptas, therefore shall not the prince of malum dwell in thee.

The foolish shall not stand in thy sight: thou hatest all workers of iniquitas.

Thou shalt destroy them that speak mendacium: the Lord will abhor the sanguinarius and deceitful man.

But as for me, I will come into thy domus in the multitude of thy misericordia: and in thy reverentia will I worship toward thy sanctuarium.

Lead me, O Lord, in thy iustitia because of mine inimicus; make thy via straight before my face.

For there is no veritas in their os; their interiora are very wickedness; their fauces are an open sepulchre; they flatter with their lingua.

Destroy them, O Deus; let them fall by their own labia: cast them out in the multitude of their transgressions; for they have rebelled against thee.

And let all those that put their fiducia in thee rejoice: let them ever shout for joy, because thou defendest them: let them also that love thy Nomen be joyful in thee.

For thou, Lord, wilt bless the iustus; with favorem wilt thou compass him as with a scutum.

O Lord, thou hast heard the desiderium of the humilis: thou wilt prepare their cor, thou wilt cause thine aurem to hear:

To judge the fatherless and the oppressus, that the vir terrae may no more afflict them.
