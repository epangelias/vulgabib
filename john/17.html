The Vulgabib

John 17   Back   Next

These words spake Iesus, and lifted up his eyes to caelum, and said, Pater, the hour is come; glorify thy Filius, that thy Filius also may glorify thee:

As thou hast given him potestatem over all carnem, that he should give aeterna vita to as many as thou hast given him.

And this is aeterna vita, that they might know thee the only verus Deus, and Iesus Christ whom thou hast sent.

I have glorified thee on terra: I have finished the opus which thou gavest me to do.

And now, O Pater, glorify thou me with thine own self with the gloria which I had with thee before the mundus was.

I have manifested thy nomen unto the homines which thou gavest me out of the mundus: thine they were, and thou gavest them me; and they have kept thy verbum.

Now they have known that all things whatsoever thou hast given me are of thee.

For I have given unto them the verba which thou gavest me; and they have received them, and have known surely that I came out from thee, and they have believed that thou didst send me.

I pray for them: I pray not for the mundus, but for them which thou hast given me; for they are thine.

And all mine are thine, and thine are mine; and I am glorified in them.

And now I am no more in the mundus, but these are in the mundus, and I come to thee. Holy Pater, keep through thy nomen those whom thou hast given me, that they may be unum, as we are.

While I was with them in the mundus, I kept them in thy nomen: those that thou gavest me I have kept, and none of them is lost, but the Filius perdition, that the Scriptura might be fulfilled.

And now come I to thee; and these things I speak in the mundus, that they might have my gaudium fulfilled in themselves.

I have given them thy verbum; and the mundus hath hated them, because they are not of the mundus, even as I am not of the mundus.

I pray not that thou shouldest take them out of the mundus, but that thou shouldest keep them from malum.

They are not of the mundus, even as I am not of the mundus.

Sanctify them through thy veritas: thy verbum is veritas.

As thou hast sent me into the mundus, even so have I also sent them into the mundus.

And for their sakes I sanctify myself, that they also might be sanctified through the veritas.

Neither pray I for these alone, but for them also which shall believe on me through their verbum;

That they all may be unum; as thou, Pater, art in me, and I in thee, that they also may be unum in us: that the mundus may believe that thou hast sent me.

And the gloria which thou gavest me I have given them; that they may be unum, even as we are unum:

I in them, and thou in me, that they may be made perfect in unum; and that the mundus may know that thou hast sent me, and hast loved them, as thou hast loved me.

Pater, I will that they also, whom thou hast given me, be with me where I am; that they may behold my gloria, which thou hast given me: for thou lovedst me before the fundamentum of the mundus.

O iustus Pater, the mundus hath not known thee: but I have known thee, and these have known that thou hast sent me.

And I have declared unto them thy nomen, and will declare it: that the amor wherewith thou hast loved me may be in them, and I in them.
