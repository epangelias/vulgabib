Psalms 41   Back   Next

Blessed is he that considereth the pauper (poor), the Lord will deliver him in the time of tribulatio (tribulation).

The Lord will preserve him, and keep him alive; and he shall be blessed upon the terra (earth): and thou wilt not deliver him unto the voluntas (will) of his enemies.

The Lord will strengthen him upon the lectus (bed) of languor (sickness): thou wilt make all his lectus (bed) in his infirmitas (sickness).

I said, Lord, have misericordia (mercy) upon me: heal my animus (soul); for I have peccatum (sinned) against thee.

Mine inimici (enemies) speak malum (evil) against me, When shall he die, and his nomen (name) perish?

And if he come to see me, he speaketh vanitas (vanity): his cor (heart) gathereth iniquitas (iniquity) to himself; he goeth forth, and speaketh in it.

All that hate me whisper together against me: against me do they devise malum (evil).

An afflictio (affliction) cleaveth fast unto him: and now that he lieth he shall rise up no more.

Yea, mine own familiaris (familiar friend), in whom I trusted, which did eat my panis (bread), hath lifted up his calcaneus (heel) against me.

But thou, O Lord, have misericordia (mercy) upon me, and raise me up, that I may render them retributio (retribution).

By this I know that thou favoreth me, because mine inimicus (enemy) doth not triumph over me.

And as for me, thou upholdest me in mine integritas (integrity), and settest me before thy facies (face) for ever.

Blessed be the Lord God of Israel from sempiternum (everlasting) to sempiternum (everlasting). Amen, and Amen.
