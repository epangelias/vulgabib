Isaiah 63   Back   Next

Who is this that cometh from Edom, with dyed vestibus, from Bozrah? this that is glorious in his vestitus, travelling in the multitude of his fortitudo?

I that speak in iustitia, mighty to salvare.

Why art thou red in thy vestibus, and thy vestitus like him that treadeth in the torcular?

I have trodden the torcular alone; and of the gentes there was none with me: for I will tread them in my ira, and trample them in my indignatio, and their sanguis shall be sprinkled upon my vestitus, and I will stain all my vestitus.

For the dies of my vindicta is come, and the anno of my redemptio.

And I looked, and there was none to help; and I wondered that there was none to uphold: therefore mine own brachium brought salvus, and my indignatio, it sustained me.

And I will tread down the populi in my ira, and make them drunk in my indignatio, and I will bring down their robur to the terra.

The clementiae of the Domini will I remember, the laudes of the Domini, because of all that the Domini hath given us, and the multa bonitas towards the domus of Israel, which he hath bestowed on them according to his misericordia, and according to the multa pietas of him.

And he said, Surely they are my populi, filii who will not prevaricare: so he was their Salvator.

In all their afflictio he was afflictus, and the Angel of his facies saved them: in his dilectio and in his clementia he redeemed them; and he bore them, and carried them all the dies of the saeculum.

But they were rebellious, and vexed his Spirit: therefore he was turned to be their inimicus, and he fought against them.

Then he remembered the dies of old, of Moses, and of his populi, saying, Where is he that brought them up out of the mare, with the pastor of his gregis? where is he that put his Spirit Sanctus within him?

That led them by the dextera of Moses with his gloria?

That divided the mare before them, to make himself a nomen sempiternum?

That led them through the abyssa, as an equus in the deserto, they stumbled not.

As the animalia that descend in the valle, the Spirit of the Domini gave them quietus.

Thus thou leddest thy populi, to make thyself a nomen gloriae.

Ne caelum et terra, et omnia quae in eis sunt, sint in aeternum, non tamen ex toto erit motus ira tua, et non erit finis sceleribus nostris.

Domine, in te speravimus, patres nostri, in te est nomen tuum, ne derelinquas nos, Domine, Deus noster.

Si iniquitates nostrae testificarentur contra nos, Domine, tu qui purgas iniquitates nostras, neque sic justificaremusur in conspectu tuo.

Facti sumus ut immundus omnes nos, et iniquitas nostra quasi pannus menstruatae, omnes iustitiae nostrae quasi folium aridum, cecidimus omnes in iniquitatem nostram, sicut folium decidit, et non est qui salvum faciat, non est qui invocet nomen tuum, qui consurgere facias, et nos suscipias, abscondisti faciem tuam a nobis, et liquisti nos in manu iniquitatis nostrae.

Et nunc, Domine, Pater noster, es tu, nos terra, opus manuum tuarum, omnes nos.

Ne irascaris, Domine, ultra, neque in aeternum memineris iniquitatis nostrae, ecce respice populum tuum, omnes nos.

Haec est desolata Sion, non est qui edificet eam, haec est Ierusalem, non est qui congreget eam, haec est civitas, et non est qui requiem det ei, haec sunt portae eius, et non est qui aperiat eas.

Fuerunt populi mei, et non est qui requirat, haec sunt vias eius, et non est qui dirigat eos, haec sunt civitates eius, et non est qui congreget eos, haec sunt portae eius, et non est qui aperiat eas, haec sunt gentes eius, et non est qui requirat eos, haec sunt domus eius, et non est qui congreget eos.

Exspectavimus, et non est qui salvus faciat, exspectavimus, et non est qui salvus faciat, exspectavimus, et non est qui dirigat nos.
