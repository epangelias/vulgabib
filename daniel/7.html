In the first year of Belshazzar rex of the Babylonians, Daniel had a somnium, and visions of his caput were troubling him.

Then the king commanded to call the magi, and the astrologi, and the sortilegi, and the Chaldeans, for to shew the king his somnium.

They came then near, and stood before the rex, who said to them, Thus is the decree; Ye shall shew me the somnium, and the interpretatio thereof.

The Chaldeans answered before the rex, and said, O rex, live for ever: tell thy servants the somnium, and we will shew the interpretatio thereof.

The rex answered and said to the Chaldeans, The decree is gone forth of me, that if ye make not known unto me the somnium, and the interpretatio thereof, ye shall be cut in pieces, and your houses shall be made a dunghill.

They answered again and said, Let the rex tell his servants the somnium, and we will shew the interpretatio thereof.

The rex answered and said, I know of certainty that ye would gain the time, because ye see the thing is gone from me.

But if ye will not make known unto me the somnium, there is but one decree for you: for ye have prepared lying and corrupt words to speak before me, till the time be changed: therefore tell me the somnium, and I shall know that ye can shew me the interpretatio thereof.

The Chaldeans answered before the rex, and said, There is not a man upon the earth that can shew the rex's mater, therefore there is no rex, rex, nor dominus, that asked such things of any magus or astrologus or Chaldea.

The king's word was, by decree, to destroy all the wise men of Babylon.

Then Daniel went in, and desired of the rex that he would give him spatium to shew the rex the interpretatio.

Then Daniel went to his house, and made the thing known to his companions, Hananiah, Mishael, and Azariah:

That they would desire mercies of the Deus of caelum concerning this secret; that Daniel and his companions should not perish with the rest of the wise men of Babylon.

Then was the secret revealed unto Daniel in a visio noctis. So Daniel blessed the Deus of caelum.

Daniel answered and said, Blessed be the name of Deus for ever and ever: for wisdom and fortitudo are his:

And he changeth the times and the seasons: he removeth rexes, and setteth up rexes: he giveth wisdom unto the wise, and knowledge to them that know understanding:

He revealeth the profundum and secret things: he knoweth what is in the tenebrae, and the lumen with him dwelleth.

I thank thee, and praise thee, O Deus of my fathers, who hast given me sapientiam and fortitudo, and hast shewed me now what we desired of thee: for thou hast now shewed us the rex's mater.

Therefore Daniel went in unto Arioch, whom the rex had ordained to destroy the wise men of Babylon: he went and said thus unto him; Destroy not the wise men of Babylon: bring me in before the rex, and I will shew unto the rex the interpretatio.

Then Arioch brought in Daniel before the rex in haste and said thus unto him, I have found a man of the captivos of Iudah, that will make known unto the rex the interpretatio.

The rex answered and said unto Daniel, whose name was Belteshazzar, Art thou able to make known unto me the somnium which I have seen, and the interpretatio thereof?

Daniel answered in the praesentia of the rex, and said, The secret which the rex hath demanded cannot the wise men, the astrologi, the magi, the diviners shew unto the rex;

But there is a Deus in caelum that revealeth secrets, and maketh known to the rex Nebuchadnezzar what shall be in the novissima diebus. Thy somnium, and the visions of thy caput upon thy bed, are these;

As for thee, O rex, thy thoughts came into thy mens upon thy bed, what should be hereafter: and he that revealeth secrets maketh known to thee what shall come to pass.

But as for me, this secret is not revealed to me for any wisdom that I have more than any living, but for their sakes that shall make known the interpretatio to the rex, and that thou mightest know the thoughts of thy cor.

Thou, O rex, sawest, and behold a great imago. This great imago, whose splendor was excellent, stood before thee; and the aspect thereof was terrible.

This imago's pedes were of ferrum, and his toes part of ferrum, and part of terra.

Thou sawest till that a lapis was cut out without hands, which smote the imago upon his pedes that were of ferrum and terra, and brake them.

Then was the ferrum, the terra, the argentum, and the aurum broken to pieces together, and became like the chaff of the area summer, and the ventus carried them away, that no place was found for them: and the lapis that smote the imago became a great mons, and filled the whole terra.

This is the somnium; and we will tell the interpretatio thereof before the rex.

Thou, O rex, art a rex of rexes: for the Deus of caelum hath given thee the regnum, potentiam, and fortitudinem, and glory;

And wheresoever the filii of men dwell, the beasts of the field and the aves of the caelum hath he given into thine hand, and hath made thee dominus of them all. Thou art this head of aurum.

And after thee shall arise another regnum inferior to thee, and another third regnum of brass, which shall bear rule over all the terra.

And the fourth regnum shall be strong as ferrum: forasmuch as ferrum breaketh in pieces and subdueth all things: and as ferrum that breaketh all these, shall it break in pieces and bruise.

And whereas thou sawest the pedes and toes, part of potters' terra, and part of ferrum, the regnum shall be divided; but there shall be in it of the fortitudo of the ferrum, forasmuch as thou sawest the ferrum mixed with miry terra.

And as the toes of the pedes were part of ferrum, and part of terra, so the regnum shall be partly strong, and partly broken.

And whereas thou sawest ferrum mixed with miry terra, they shall mingle themselves with the semen of men: but they shall not cleave one to another, even as ferrum is not mixed with terra.

Now in the diebus of these rexes shall the Deus of caelum set up a regnum, which shall never be destroyed: and the regnum shall not be left to other people, but it shall break in pieces and consume all these regna, and it shall stand for ever.

Forasmuch as thou sawest that the lapis was cut out of the mons without hands, and that it brake in pieces the ferrum, the terra, the argentum, and the aurum; the great Deus hath made known to the rex what shall come to pass hereafter: and the somnium is certain, and the interpretatio thereof sure.

Then the rex Nebuchadnezzar fell upon his face, and worshipped Daniel, and commanded that they should offer him oblations and sweet odors.

The rex answered unto Daniel, and said, Of a truth it is, that your Deus is a Deus of dei, the Dominus of rexes, and a revealer of secrets, seeing thou couldest reveal this secret.

Then the rex made Daniel a great man, and gave him many dona, and made him ruler over the whole provincia of Babylon, and chief of the governors over all the wise men of Babylon.

Then Daniel requested of the rex, and he set Shadrach, Meshach, and Abednego, over the affairs of the provincia of Babylon: but Daniel sat in the porta of the rex.
