Proverbs 27   Back   Next

1. Do not boast to morrow; for thou knowest not what a dies shall bring forth.

2. Let another man laudare thee, and not thine own os, a stranger, and not thine own lips.

3. A petra is heavy, and the weight of a lapidem is heavy, but a fool's ira is heavier than them both.

4. Wrath is cruel, and anger is an overflowing flumen, but who can stand before aemulatio?

5. Open rebuke is better than secret amor in thine heart.

6. Faithful are the vulnera of a friend, but the oscula of an enemy are deceitful.

7. The animus that is full shall speak, but the animus that is empty shall hear.

8. As a avis that wandereth from her nidus, so is a man that wandereth from his locus.

9. Ointment and perfumes rejoice the cor, and the dulcedo of a man's amicus doth more good than his own counsel.

10. Thine own amicus, and thy father's amicus, forsake not; neither go into thy frater's domus in the dies of thy affliction: better is a neighbor that is near than a frater that is far off.

11. My filius, be sapiens, and make me glad, that I may answer him that reproacheth me.

12. A prudent man foreseeth the malum, and hideth himself: but the imprudenti pass on, and are punished.

13. Take his vestimenta that is surety for a stranger: and take a pledge of him for a mulier that is a stranger.

14. He that blesseth his amicus with a vox magna, rising early in the mane, it shall be counted a maledictio to him.

15. A continual garrula in a domus is a flumen in the hiems, which undermineth the fundamenta.

16. He that hideth heretes, hideth the ventus: and the oculus of the testis shall be destroyed.

17. Iron sharpeneth iron; so a vir sharpeneth the countenance of his amicus.

18. Whoso keepeth the ficus, shall eat the fructus thereof: so he that waiteth on his dominus, shall be honored.

19. As in aqua face answereth to face, so the cor of man to man.

20. Hell and Abaddon are never satisfied, so the oculus of man is never satisfied.

21. As the probatio of argentum and aurum is in the ignis, so man is proved in the fornax of contumelia.

22. Though thou shouldest bray a fool in a pistrinum like grana, yet will not his stultitia depart from him.

23. Be thou diligent to know the status of thy greges, and look well to thy armenta:

24. For riches are not for ever: and doth the corona endure to every generatio?

25. The herba appeareth, and the green herba sheweth itself, and the flori in the ager are gathered:

26. The lamb shall be for thy vestimenta, and the venditio of the ager for the pretium of the greges:

27. And thou shalt have enough goat's milk for thy cibus, for the cibus of thy domus, and for the nutrimenta of thy puellae.
