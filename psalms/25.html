Psalms 25   Back   Next

Unto thee, O Deus, do I lift up my animus: my Deus, I have trusted in thee, let me not be confused, neither let mine inimici triumph over me.

Yea, let none that wait on thee be ashamed: let them be ashamed which transgress without causa.

Shew me thy vias, O Deus; teach me thy semitas.

Lead me forth in thy veritas, and doce me: for thou art Deus my Salvator; on thee do I wait all the die.

Remember, O Deus, thy misericordiam and thy amor: for they have been from sempiternum.

Remember not the peccata of my iuventus and my delicta: according to thy misericordiam remember thou me for thy bonitas' sake, O Deus.

Gratiosus and iustus is Deus: propterea he will doce peccatores in the via.

He will guide the mansuetos in iudicium: and he will doce the mansuetos in his via.

All the vias of Deus are misericordiam and veritas to such as keep his pactum and testimonium.

Quia thy nomine's sake, O Deus, I pray thee, be propitius unto my peccatum: for it is multum.

Quis est homo that feareth Deus, him shall he teach in the via that he shall choose.

His animus shall rest in bonitas: and his semen shall inherit terram.

The secretum of Deus is with them that feareth him; and he will shew them his pactum.

Mine oculi are ever toward Deus: for he shall pluck my pedes out of laqueus.

Respice in me, and misere me: for I am solus and pauper.

The angustiae of my cor have increased: deliver me out of my angustiae.

Look upon mine afflictio and my labor: and forgive all my peccata.

Consider mine inimici: for they are multus; and they have hated me with odium implacabile.

Keep my animus and my corpus: for I have trusted in thee.

Let integrity and equitas preserve me: for I wait on thee.

Redime Israel, O Deus, out of all his afflictiones.
