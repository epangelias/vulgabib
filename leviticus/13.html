Leviticus 13   Back   Next

When a man shall have in the skin of his corpus a rising, a scab, or a bright spot, and it be in the skin of his corpus like the plague of leprosy; then he shall be brought unto Aaron the sacerdos, or unto one of his filii.

And the sacerdos shall look on the plague in the skin of the corpus: and when the hair in the plague is turned white, and the plague in sight be deeper than the skin of his corpus, it is a plague of leprosy: and the sacerdos shall look on him, and pronounce him immundus.

If the bright spot be white in the skin of his corpus, and in sight be not deeper than the skin, and the hair thereof be not turned white; then the sacerdos shall shut up him that hath the plague seven dies.

And the sacerdos shall look on him the seventh dies: and, behold, if the plague in his own sight be at a stand, and the plague spread not in the skin; then the sacerdos shall shut him up seven dies more:

And the sacerdos shall look on him again the seventh dies: and, behold, if the plague be somewhat dark, and the plague spread not in the skin, the sacerdos shall pronounce him mundus: it is but a scab: and he shall wash his vestes, and be mundus.

But if the scab spread much abroad in the skin, after that he hath been seen of the sacerdos for his cleansing, he shall be seen of the sacerdos again:

And if the sacerdos see that, behold, the scab covereth all his corpus, he shall pronounce him mundus that hath the plague: it is his lepra, and he is mundus.

When a man shall have his corpus full of lepra, and the sacerdos see him, then he shall pronounce him immundus, because the plague is in all his corpus, so he shall be put apart; he dwelleth alone; without the castrum shall his habitation be.

And the vestis that hath the plague of lepra, whether it be a vestis of lana, or a vestis of lini, or in the warp, or woof, or any thing of pelles, or any thing that is made of pelles;

If the plague be greenish or reddish in the vestis, or in the warp, or in the woof, or in any thing of pelles, or in any instrument of pelles; behold, it is a plague of lepra, and he shall shew it unto the sacerdos:

And the sacerdos shall see the plague, and he shall shut up that which hath the plague seven dies:

And he shall see the plague on the seventh dies: if the plague be spread in the vestis, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in the pelles, or in any thing made of pelles, the plague is a fretting lepra; it is immundus.

He shall therefore burn that vestis, whether warp, or woof, in lana, or in lini, or any thing of pelles, wherein the plague is: for it is a fretting lepra; it shall be burnt in the ignis.

And if the sacerdos shall look, and, behold, the plague be not spread in the vestis, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in any thing of pelles;

Then the sacerdos shall command that they wash the thing wherein the plague is, and he shall shut it up seven dies more:

And the sacerdos shall look on the plague after that it is washed: and, behold, if the plague have not changed his color, and the plague be not spread; it is immundus; thou shalt burn it in the ignis; for it is fretting, in the outside of it, or in the inside.

If the sacerdos look, that, behold, the plague be somewhat dark after the washing of it; then he shall rend it out of the vestis, or out of the pelles, or out of the warp, or out of the woof:

And if it appear still in the vestis, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in any thing of pelles; it is a spreading plague: thou shalt burn that wherein the plague is in the ignis.

But the vestis, either warp, or woof, or whatsoever thing of pelles, if thou shalt wash it when the plague is out of it; then it shall be washed the second time, and shall be mundus.

This shall be the lex of the plague of lepra in a vestis of woolen or linnen, either in the warp, or woof, or any thing of pelles, to pronounce it mundus, or immundus.
