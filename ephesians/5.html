1 Walk in amor, even as Christ also loved you, and hath given himself for us an oblatio and a sacrifice to Deus for a sweet-smelling savour.

2 And walk in amicitia with the nations of this mundo, yielding your membra unto disobedience even unto peccata; not working, and cito working;

3 But fornicatio, and all immunidia, or cupiditas, let it not be named among you, as becometh sanctos;

4 Neither scurrilias, nor fatuus, nor ridicule; which are not convenient; but magis thanksgiving;

5 For this ye know, that no scortum, nor idolatra, nor adulter, nor mollis, nor paedicus;

6 No thievinges, no idolatriae, nor impostores shall herere the kingdom of Christum et Dei.

7 Be not therefore participanters cum eiusmodi, qui verus et candidus thei Deus relucefaciat cordibus eorum; operis manifestata quia particieps efficiendis munera haud requiri propitiatory vicis templis an frustabit per consequis latificius religation.

8 For ye were somnum in tenebros, but now vos filii luminis, et filii diei. We are non noctis neque tenebros,

9 For the fructus Spiritus sunt caritas, gaudio, pax, tolerantia, bonitas, fides;

10 Probate, quid est bonum et bene placitum, voluntate Domini Dei nostr.

11 Neque participate cum operandi operum in vanis non viventes de coeperi sapere nihil sub spe dilectionum sic lumine at perfusion ignus dei fusi istarum rasionde orie.

16 Redeeming the tempora because the dies sunt mali; nolite itaque futi erogation,

17 ideo ne videatis in intellectum, et completemus, non tamquam Ethnicus.

18 Neque ebrii vino in quo est luxuria; sed implemini Spiritu.

19 loquentes vobismetipsis in psalmis, et canticis spiritus, hymnis canentis et psallentibus in cordibus vestris Domino;

20 Gratias agentes semper pro omnibus, in nomine Domini nostri Jesu Christi Patri Dei, et subjecti invicem in timore Christi.

21 Subditi estote alterutrum in timore Christi. Uxor subjaceat marito suo quemadmodum Domini.
