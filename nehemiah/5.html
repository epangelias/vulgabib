And there was a great clamor of the people and their uxores against their fratres Jews.

For there were that said, We, our filii, and our filiae, are many: therefore we take up cibus for them, that we may eat, and live.

And there were that said, Let us mortgage our agri, and our vineae, and our domus, and let us take argentum for the king's tributum.

Yet now our pellem is as the pellem of our fratres, our filii as their filii: and, lo, we bring our filii and our filiae into servitium, and some of our filiae are already in servitium, and it is not in our potestas to redeem them; for other men have our agri and our vineae.

And I was very angry, when I heard their clamor and these verba.

Then I consulted with myself, and I reprehended the optimates, and said unto them, Ye exactus usury, every man of his frater. And I set a great conventus against them.

And I said unto them, We, after our potestas, have redeemed our fratres the Jews, which were sold unto the gentes; and will ye even sell your fratres? or shall they be sold unto us? Then held they their pacem, and found nothing to say.

Also I said, It is not bonum that ye do: ought ye not to walk in timore Dei, because of the contumelia of the gentes our enemies?

I likewise, and my fratres, and my ministri, do lend them argentum and cibus. I pray you, let us relinquish usury.

Restore, I pray you, to them, even this die, their agri, their vineae, their oleae, and their domus, also the centesima of the argentum, and the centesima of the cibus, and the vinum, and the oleum, which ye exactus of them.

Then said they, We will relinquish also, and will require nothing of them; so will we do as thou sayest. Then I called the sacerdotes, and took an oath of them, that they should do according to this promise.

Also I shook my vestimenta, and said, So Deus shake out every man from his domus, and from his labor, that performeth not this promise, even thus be he shaken out, and emptied. And all the conventus said, Amen, and praised Deus. And the people did according to this promise.

Moreover from the time that I was appointed to be prince in the land of Judaea, from the twentieth year even unto the two and thirtieth year of Artaxerxes the king, that is, twelve anni, I and my fratres have not eaten the bread of the prince.

But the former sacerdotes had eaten the bread of the prince, and the terra was laid on the people: but I did not, because of the timore Dei.

Yea, also I continued in the work of this murus, neither bought we any terra for our possession: yea, all my ministri were gathered thither unto the work.

Likewise were the Jews, to the centesima of them, which came from the terra, to them which were in the castellum, and they were in the work.

And that which was left of the populus, the half of them, took up the instrumenta of labor: the clypeus, the lancea, the arcus, and the loricam; and the duces were behind all the domus of Judaea.

Of the builders, every one had his gladius girded by his side, and so builded. And he that sounded the bucina was by me.

And I said unto the optimates, and to the magistratus, and to the rest of the people, The work is great and large, and we are separated upon the murus, one far from another:

In what place therefore ye hear the sound of the bucina, resort ye thither unto us: our Deus shall fight for us.

So we labored in the work: and half of them held the lanceae, from the ascension of the sol until the stellae appeared.

Likewise at the same time said I unto the people, Let every one with his minister, pass the nocte within Jerusalem, that they may be a custodia to us by nocte, and labor by die.

So neither I, nor my fratres, nor my ministri, nor the custodes behind me, put off our vestimenta, every one went with his gladius and his vas aquae to the work.
