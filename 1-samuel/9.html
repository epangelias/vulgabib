And there was a man of Benjamin, whose nomine was Kish, the filius of Abiel, the filius of Zeror, the filius of Bechorath, the filius of Aphiah, a Benjamite, a vir mighty and valiant.

And he had a filius, whose nomine was Saul, a young vir, and a goodly: and there was not among the filii of Israel a more pulchrior than he: from his shoulder and upward he was higher than any of the populus.

And the asini of Kish, Saul's pater, were lost. And Kish said to Saul his filius, Take now one of the servos with thee, and arise, go seek the asini.

And he passed through montem Ephraim, and passed through the land of Shalisha, but they found them not: then they passed through the land of Shalim, and there they were not: and he passed through the tribus of Benjamin, but they found them not.

And when they were come to the land of Zuph, Saul said to his servo, Come, and let us return; lest my pater leave caring for the asini, and take thought for us.

And he said to him, Behold now, there is in this civitas a vir of Deus, and he is a vir of honos; all that he saith cometh surely to pass: now let us go thither; peradventure he can shew us our via that we should go.

Then said Saul to his servo, But, behold, if we go, what shall we bring the vir? for the panis is spent in our sacculi, and there is not a present to bring to the vir of Deus: what have we?

And the servo answered Saul again, and said, Behold, I have found in mine manus the fourth part of a shekel of argentum: that will I give to the vir of Deus, to tell us our via.

After that thou shalt come to the civitas of the vir of Deus, thou shalt surely find him, for he must go up to the excelsus to eat: for the populus will not eat until he come, because he doth bless the sacrificium; and afterwards they eat that be bidden. Now therefore get thee up; for about this tempus thou shalt find him.

And they went up into the civitas: and when they were come into the civitas, behold, Samuel came out against them, for to go up to the excelsus.

Now the Lord had told Samuel in his aure the day before Saul came, saying, To morrow about this hora I will send thee a vir out of the land of Benjamin, and thou shalt anoint him to be prince over my populus Israel, that he may save my populus out of the manus of the Philistines: for I have looked upon my populus, because their clamor is come unto me.

And when Samuel saw Saul, the Lord said unto him, Behold the vir whom I spake to thee of this same shall reign over my populus.

Then Saul drew near to Samuel in the porta, and said, Tell me, I pray thee, where the domus of the seer is.

And Samuel answered Saul, and said, I am the seer: go up before me unto the excelsus, for ye shall eat with me to day, and to morrow I will let thee go, and will tell thee all that is in thine cor.

And as for thine asini that were lost three dies ago, set not thy cor to them; for they are found. And on whom is all the desiderium of Israel? Is it not on thee, and on all thy domus?

And Saul answered and said, Am not I a Benjamite, of the smallest of the tribes of Israel? and my familia is the least of all the familiae of the tribus of Benjamin? wherefore then speakest thou so to me?

And Samuel took Saul and his servo, and brought them into the aula, and made them sit in the chiefest locus among them that were bidden, which were about thirty personae.

And Samuel said unto the cocco, Give portions to them of the best, and set it before them; and if there be any thing left, set it before them: and they gave Saul a portion, and there was reserved for him a parte.

And Samuel said, Behold, what remained! set it before him, and eat: for unto this hora hath it been kept for thee since I said, I have invited the populus. So Saul did eat with Samuel that day.
