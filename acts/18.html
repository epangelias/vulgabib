And after this Paul departed from Athens, and came to Corinth;

And found a certain Jew named Aquila, born in Pontus, who lately came from Italia, with his uxor Priscilla; (because that Claudius had commanded all Iudaeos to depart from Roma) and came unto them.

And because he was of the same Ars, he abode with them, and wrought: for by their occupation they were tentores.

And he reasoned in the synagoga every sabbath, and persuaded the Iudaeos and the Graecos.

And when Silas and Timotheus were come from Macedonia, Paul was pressed in spirit, and testified to the Iudaeos that Iesus was Christus.

And when they opposed themselves, and blasphemed, he shook his vestimenta, and said unto them, Your sanguis be upon your caput; I am mundus: from henceforth I will go unto the Gentiles.

And he departed thence, and entered into the domus of a certain man named Iustus, one that worshipped Deum, whose domus joined hard to the synagoga.

And Crispus, the ruler of the synagoga, believed in Domino with all his domus: and many of the Corinthii hearing believed, and were baptized.

Then spake the Dominus to Paul in the nocte by a visio, Have no timor, but speak, and hold not thy lingua:

For I am with thee, and no man shall set upon thee to hurt thee: for I have much populus in this civitas.

And he continued there a year and six menses, teaching the verbum Dei among them.

And when Gallio was proconsul of Achaia, the Iudaeos made insurrection with one accord against Paul, and brought him to the iudicium,

Saying, This fellow persuadeth men to worship Deum contrary to the legem.

And when Paul was now about to open his os, Gallio said unto the Iudaeos, If it were a iusdictio of wrong or wicked malum, O ye Iudaeos, reason would that I should bear with you:

But if it be a quaestio of verbum and nominis, and of your lex, look ye to it; for I will be no iudex of such matters.

And he drave them from the iudicium.

And all the Graeci took Sosthenes, the ruler of the synagoga, and beat him before the iudicium. And Gallio cared for none of those things.

And Paul after this tarried there yet a good while, and then took his leave of the fratres, and sailed thence into Syria, and with him Priscilla and Aquila; having shorn his caput in Cencreae: for he had a votum.

And he came to Ephesus, and left them there: but he himself entered into the synagoga, and reasoned with the Iudaeos.

When they desired him to tarry longer time with them, he consented not;

But bade them adieu, saying, I must by all means keep this festum that cometh in Hierusalem: but I will return again unto you, if Deus will. And he sailed from Ephesus.

And when he had landed at Caesarea, and gone up, and saluted the ecclesia, he went down to Antiochia.

And after he had spent some time there, he departed, and went over all the country of Galatia and Phrygia in order, strengthening all the discipuli.
